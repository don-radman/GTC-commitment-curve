# Principles

The design ethos that shapes every play the agent generates. These are guidelines, not strict rules. Use them as a compass, not a checklist. When the project context or the persona warrants creative deviation, deviate, and note why.

---

## 1. Lean intrinsic on rewards

Recognition, status, access, and identity beat tokens, points, and cash in almost every community context. Extrinsic rewards have a place when used strategically (a finite launch incentive, a season-end moment, a paid bug bounty), but they're the default move that's too often abused in web3 community work. Reach for intrinsic first. Reach for extrinsic only when the play genuinely calls for it.

## 2. Tiny first asks

The first play in the Discovering stage should clear Fogg's Action Line for any persona, regardless of motivation level. Five seconds, one click, one tap. If the member needs to think about whether to do it, it's already too big.

## 3. Project-specific where you can, generic where you have to

Use the project's actual surface area (Discord channels, Telegram groups, in-product features, real product names) when the user has given you material to work with. Don't invent brand vocabulary, made-up program names, or features that don't exist. When in doubt, describe the play in functional terms ("a private channel for top contributors") rather than fabricated terms ("the Inner Circle Vault™").

## 4. Sequencing over intensity

A boring ask delivered at the right moment beats a thrilling ask delivered at the wrong moment. The curve is a sequencing tool, not an excitement tool. Each play should logically follow from the one before it and obviously set up the one after.

## 5. Belonging compounds

Most communities under-invest in belonging in stages 1 and 2. Members who don't feel seen by the end of stage 2 don't proceed. Default to plays that produce a moment of recognition early, regardless of the persona's stated dominant motivator.

## 6. Design for the trough, not the peak

Whatever the agent designs has to work on a tired Tuesday, not just on launch day. Motivation is a wave, ability is fragile. Plays that depend on members showing up "energized and excited" will mostly fail.

## 7. Creativity inside the structure

Within the constraints (4 stages, 3 plays per stage, escalating friction, mechanic cap of 3), the agent should be creative. Different projects deserve different curves. Different personas deserve different curves. If a play is structurally sound and intrinsically motivated, err toward more interesting and more specific over safer and more generic.
